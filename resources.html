BirthControl

Birth control, or contraception, refers to methods

and devices used to prevent pregnancy.

It includes hormonal options like pills,

patches,and IUDs, as well as barrier methods,

sterilization, and fertility awareness techniques.

Tampon

Tampons are feminine hygiene products used

during menstruation. They're inserted into the

vaginal canal to absorb menstrual blood. They

offer convenience and comfort, with various

absorbency levels to suit individual needs.

STI

Sexually transmitted infections (STIs) are

infections transmitted through sexual contact.

They include diseases like chlamydia,

gonorrhea and HIV. STIs can have serious

health consequences and require medical

attention and safe sexual practices for prevention.

Gender Identity

Gender identity is a deeply personal sense of

being male, female, a blend of both, or neither.

It may not necessarily align with one's assigned

sex at birth, and respecting individual identities

is important.

Puberty

Puberty is a natural process of physical and

hormonal changes during adolescence. It

involves the development of secondary sexual

characteristics and signifies the transition

from childhood to adulthood.

Hormonal Imbalance

Hormonal imbalance refers to irregularities in

hormone production and regulation. It can lead

to various health issues, including mood swings,

weight changes, and menstrual irregularities, and

may require medical intervention.
